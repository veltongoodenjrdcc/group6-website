Main Group Project · Ques 2

Project choice and value rationale

By consensus, choose a project for any Jamaican business for implementation by the Group and say how the Group decided the idea would add value to the company.

By consensus, Group 6 selected the redevelopment and modernisation of FindYello.com for Yello Media Group. The proposed project is to reposition FindYello from a legacy online directory into a modern, mobile-first, AI-assisted local business discovery and lead-conversion platform for Jamaica and the wider Caribbean.

The upgraded platform would prioritise cleaner search, verified business profiles, better data quality, mobile usability, map and WhatsApp integration, reviews, category pages, and advertiser analytics.

Section 04 · Environmental Scan

FindYello.com project scan

This scan combines external and internal analysis because strategic management requires identifying opportunities, threats, strengths, and weaknesses before choosing a practical strategy.

Context Internal PESTEL Competition Forces SWOT

Company and strategic context

Yello Media Group is a multimedia marketing company serving the Caribbean, Latin America and the Channel Islands. Its website states that it serves 19 countries and 32,000+ customers. Its LinkedIn profile describes a legacy network of print telephone directories, online properties, unique visitors, and annual searches.

The company began from the traditional telephone directory/Yellow Pages model. Search engines, smartphones, social media, Google Maps, online reviews and WhatsApp have changed how people discover and contact businesses, weakening the old advantage of owning the printed directory.

Internal environmental scan

The internal scan shows that FindYello has brand recognition, regional scale, and existing business relationships, but the project must address platform weakness, strategic dilution, and data-quality risk.

Internal Area Finding Strategic Meaning
Brand equity FindYello benefits from strong legacy recognition from the Yellow Pages and telephone-directory tradition. This creates trust and awareness that newer directory platforms may not have.
Regional scale Yello has operated across multiple Caribbean and related markets and publicly states that it serves 19 countries and 32,000+ customers. The platform can be positioned as a regional local-discovery network, not only a Jamaica directory.
Existing data and relationships Yello already has business listings, categories, advertisers and client relationships. The redevelopment can build on existing data and advertiser relationships rather than starting from zero.
Product and platform weakness Group-provided internal context indicates that the platform has suffered from outdated systems, slow product development and an overly sales-led approach. The project must include product governance, user research, a product roadmap, analytics and continuous improvement.
Strategic dilution Yello previously offered broad digital marketing services including websites, social media management and SEM. Broad services can produce revenue, but they may distract from FindYello as the company's strongest proprietary asset.
Data quality risk Directories lose value when phone numbers, locations, opening hours, categories, photos and business status are inaccurate or duplicated. Listing verification, clean data and easy self-service updates should be core priorities.

External macro scan: PESTEL

The PESTEL scan identifies the broad conditions shaping the project: SME digital transformation, cautious marketing spend, mobile consumer expectations, global platform competition, paper-light opportunities, and data-protection obligations.

Factor Key Issues Implication for FindYello
Political / Governmental Regional governments and business-support bodies continue to encourage digital transformation and SME modernisation. FindYello can position itself as infrastructure for business discovery and SME visibility across Jamaica and the Caribbean.
Economic Businesses need affordable customer acquisition, but inflation and operating costs make them more cautious with marketing spend. FindYello must prove return on investment through analytics such as profile views, calls, WhatsApp clicks, map directions and lead indicators.
Social Jamaica is highly connected, with high internet penetration and heavy social media use. Users expect fast, mobile-friendly discovery with contact details, maps, social links, reviews, photos and instant messaging.
Technological Google Business Profile is free and places businesses in Google Search and Maps. Social platforms and map tools also shape discovery. FindYello must compete through local relevance, verified data, AI-assisted search, stronger category pages and advertiser insights.
Environmental A stronger digital platform reduces dependence on printed directories, but digital platforms should remain accessible on older devices and slower data connections. The redevelopment should support a paper-light model while keeping the platform fast, efficient and inclusive.
Legal Jamaica's data-protection environment requires attention to registration, data protection officers where applicable, annual impact assessments and the eight data standards. If FindYello expands reviews, analytics, AI search and user accounts, it must include privacy, consent, moderation, accuracy and security controls.

Competitive environment

FindYello competes with global search platforms, social media, WhatsApp, tourism review platforms, free directories, and newer locally positioned directory products.

Competitor / Substitute Evidence Strategic Impact
Google Business Profile / Google Maps Businesses can create a free Business Profile and appear in Google Search and Maps. The strongest substitute. It is free, familiar and tied to where many consumers already search.
Social media and WhatsApp Jamaica has heavy social platform use, and many businesses use messaging as a practical online presence. Reduces dependence on directories and raises expectations for instant contact.
JamaicaIndex JamaicaIndex presents category-based business directory coverage with advanced search options. A direct local directory competitor with category-based discovery.
WorkandJam WorkandJam offers Jamaica business search by category, map, parish/island and local business categories. Competes by being locally oriented and map/category focused.
ZipLeaf Jamaica ZipLeaf describes itself as one of Jamaica's largest online business directories and offers free business listing. Competes for businesses seeking simple, free visibility.
Ezy Web Pro Directory Ezy Web Pro positions its directory around verified listings, WhatsApp buttons, Google Maps integration, dedicated URLs, mobile-friendly design and schema markup. Shows how newer competitors are selling modern features that FindYello must match or exceed.
TripAdvisor and tourism platforms Tourism-focused platforms carry review weight and tourist traffic for restaurants, attractions, hotels, tours and experiences. Strong substitute for tourism and hospitality categories.

Industry forces summary

The market is attractive because local discovery remains important, but the competitive pressure is high. FindYello must differentiate through trust, local data quality, usability, and measurable advertiser value.

Force Strength Rationale
Threat of substitutes High Google Search, Google Maps, social media, WhatsApp, TripAdvisor and free directories already satisfy many discovery needs.
Competitive rivalry High FindYello competes with global platforms, local directories and digital agencies for business visibility budgets and consumer attention.
Buyer power High SMEs can choose free or low-cost alternatives and will demand proof that paid listings produce leads.
Threat of new entrants Medium Directory pages are easy to launch, but building trusted data, traffic, reviews and advertiser relationships is harder.
Supplier power Medium Technology vendors, hosting providers, data providers and AI/search tools matter, but Yello can reduce dependency through internal product capability.

SWOT summary

Strengths

  • Strong legacy brand recognition.
  • Regional footprint.
  • Existing listings and advertiser relationships.
  • History in local search.
  • Known Caribbean business-information role.

Weaknesses

  • Outdated platform experience.
  • Possible data-quality issues.
  • Slow product development.
  • Overextension into broad service lines.
  • Weaker relevance among younger users who default to Google and social media.

Opportunities

  • AI-assisted local search.
  • Verified listings.
  • WhatsApp and map integration.
  • Analytics for advertisers.
  • Regional category pages.
  • Listing management across wider platform networks.

Threats

  • Google Business Profile and Maps.
  • Social media discovery.
  • Free directory competitors.
  • Fake reviews and moderation risk.
  • Data-protection obligations.
  • Customer distrust caused by inaccurate or outdated listings.

Section 05 · Recommendation

Strategic implications and recommendation

The scan shows that FindYello's problem is not awareness. The core issue is relevance: whether consumers and businesses see FindYello as faster, more accurate, more trusted and more useful than the alternatives.

Priority 01

Modernise the product

Improve mobile performance, search, business profiles, maps, WhatsApp/contact buttons, reviews and intuitive navigation.

Priority 02

Rebuild data trust

Prioritise verification, duplicate removal, updated categories and easy profile updates for business owners.

Priority 03

Add AI-assisted discovery

Support natural-language searches such as "find a plumber near Portmore open today" or "family lawyer in Kingston with reviews."

Priority 04

Prove advertiser value

Show views, calls, direction requests, WhatsApp clicks, website clicks and other lead indicators in advertiser dashboards.

Final value case

This project would add value by turning Yello's historic directory advantage into a modern local-discovery advantage. It would help consumers find reliable businesses faster and help businesses gain measurable visibility, credibility and customer leads.
